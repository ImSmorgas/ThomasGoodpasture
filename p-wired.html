Project Overview

My deep affection for my city, Chattanooga, is surpassed only by my admiration for the flourishing small businesses that contribute to its warmth and friendliness. These local establishments are the heartbeat of our community. One such favorite of mine is Wired Coffee Bar, which has been a cherished spot since its opening in Ooltewah back in 2014.

One observation I've made is that many of these small businesses have websites that may not reflect their unique charm. Eager to contribute to the enhancement of my city, I aim to utilize my web design skills to bring polish and personality to these businesses' online presence. My mission for this project is clear â€“ to create websites that exude personality, exhibit polish, and prioritize usability. I believe this can empower local businesses to not only stand out but also compete effectively against larger chains.

Problems Recognized

01. Improper Showcase of Products

Wired Coffee Bar's existing website lacks a dedicated menu. Currently, the only available menu is on a mobile ordering platform, which does not comprehensively showcase all their offerings. The "choose how you want to get wired" section, while informative about drink styles, falls short in presenting the full array of products. It primarily focuses on hot, iced, frozen, and tea options, neglecting the diverse and delightful range of food and specialty drinks that Wired has to offer.

02. Unpolished Top-bar Navigation System

The top bar navigation on the website is deficient in several key UI design principles. Notably, it lacks crucial elements such as alignment, balance, and emphasis. Moreover, its functionality falls short as it primarily redirects users to a similar location that could be more effectively presented in a dedicated about or contact page. Enhancing the alignment, balance, and emphasis in the navigation, while reorganizing content to more appropriate sections, would contribute to a more user-friendly and aesthetically pleasing website.

03. Abnormally Large Header Images

The unusually sized headers scattered across the pages are visually jarring. They draw attention to broader image sizing issues throughout the website, highlighting inconsistencies in uniformity and alignment. Addressing these concerns would significantly enhance the overall visual coherence and user experience on the site.

04. Distinct Lack of UI Design Principles

The website is currently lacking in the application of essential UI design principles. Notably, alignment and balance stand out as significant visual discrepancies. The overall impression is akin to a webpage hanging on by a crooked thread, with only a hint of excitement to loosely bind everything together. Addressing these issues and incorporating a thoughtful implementation of the design principles will contribute to a more cohesive and visually engaging website.

Solutions Found

UI Changes

Beginning from the top, a comprehensive redesign of the navbar has been implemented. This includes the integration of the company logo, the addition of new web pages, and the incorporation of active and hover styling for improved user interaction. The previously oversized headers have been replaced with a more user-friendly Jumbotron-style header, providing a familiar and welcoming impression. Directly below, a section has been introduced to offer insights into the company's commitment to quality. Immediately following, a strategic page redirect has been included, allowing users to seamlessly navigate to the menu and explore the full range of products offered by the company.

Changes to Come

The addition of a story section to the homepage is in progress. However, its current integration feels disjointed due to a lack of contrast. As part of the ongoing Wired project, I am actively brainstorming additional sections that would contribute valuable information. This strategic approach aims to introduce more separation between the existing sections, ensuring a more cohesive and visually pleasing layout. Stay tuned for further updates as the project evolves.

Addition of Menu

In designing the menu, my goal was to evoke the appearance of items presented on a sheet of paper. I opted for a card system that incorporates essential information such as an image, title, price, and description. Due to the use of stock images, maintaining a consistent brand focus posed challenges during the menu creation. However, the setup is flexible and allows for easy adjustments if necessary, ensuring adaptability as the project progresses.
