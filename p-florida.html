Project Overview

As part of a recent school assignment, my six classmates and I collaborated on a team project. With a total of seven people, each team member was tasked with creating their own page for the site. The project had intentionally minimal requirements to allow for creative freedom and a shared vision among the team.

The key requirements included creating mockups of our respective sections using Figma, ensuring representation for desktop, mobile, and tablet views. The ultimate goal was to align the final version of our website closely with the initial mockups. This approach allowed us the flexibility to explore and implement our ideas while maintaining a cohesive and visually appealing end result.

Desired Learning Objectives

01. Collaborate Efficiently in a Team Environment

Given the chance to collaborate within a team of designers, I proactively sought to make a meaningful impact on the project. Recognizing the importance of teamwork, I focused on practicing key factors to enhance our collective effectiveness. This included holding both myself and my team members accountable, maintaining open and effective communication, empowering teammates when needed, and ensuring a shared vision across all sections.

Through these intentional actions during collaborative moments, trust and harmony were fostered within the team. My goal was to not only contribute individually but also to cultivate an environment where everyone felt valued, appreciated, and aligned towards a common goal.

Early Design Process

My role

The team was so impressed with the attention to detail that I showcased with my Visit Beautiful Chattanooga project, that they wanted me to create them a layout template for the project. We all adhered to similar styles of layouts on our individual pages. The showcased image represents an early rendition of the layout shared with the team. To enhance understanding, I added comments throughout the code, offering detailed explanations for each section.

In one of our meetings, I observed a fellow student struggling to grasp the intricacies of my code. In response, I created a more detailed variation, guiding her through the intricacies and providing clear explanations. This approach proved effective, enabling her to swiftly grasp the concepts and contribute a page that seamlessly aligned with the rest of our project.

Color Palette

The team leader took the lead in making color palette decisions for our project. We opted for a bold approach, departing from basic colors that many of us had previously used on our websites. The chosen colors drew inspiration from fruits, resulting in a rich, plump purple, a vibrant orange, and a light, lemony yellow. This selection contributed to a visually cohesive and engaging look for the entire site.

Finishing Design Process

Mockups

These mockups showcase my contribution to the project. Despite not being the team lead, I took it upon myself to alleviate the workload for my fellow students. Fueled by a surge of creativity and motivation to finish the semester strongly, I recognized that some team members were grappling with a demanding schedule. In response, I willingly shouldered the additional responsibility of creating both the home page and the Orlando page.

In my perspective, a crucial aspect of teamwork is offering assistance whenever possible. Witnessing some teammates feeling overwhelmed by the busy semester, I felt compelled to be as supportive as I could. I understand the challenges of feeling stuck in the design and development process, so I make it a priority to uplift my teammates promptly whenever I sense stagnation.
