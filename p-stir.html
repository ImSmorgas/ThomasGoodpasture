Project Overview

Throughout my tenure as a server at STIR, I gained valuable insights into the challenges users faced with the mobile website. In the latter half of the Covid-19 pandemic, with traditional menus out of circulation, guests resorted to scanning QR codes at their tables for menu access through the website. It was during this pivotal time that I embarked on my first UX Design assignment - crafting mockups for a local restaurant's website.

Armed with a wealth of user feedback, I delved into enhancing an already stellar design. The mission: address user pain points and elevate the overall user experience.

Tools Used

Problems Recognized

01.

The primary challenge identified by users was the difficulty in locating specific sections of the menu. The STIR website featured an auto-scrolling function designed to guide users to their intended destination. Depending on the time of day, selecting the "menu" option triggered a scrolling event directing users to the menu relevant to that specific time. However, the destination it scrolled to was positioned beyond the menu selectors. As a result, guests often found themselves seeking assistance in locating items such as desserts, cocktails, and more.

02.

Another notable challenge was the dropdown menu's struggle with overcrowding, resulting in a lack of spacing and uniformity. The social media icon links, crucial for engagement, were obscured due to limited space. Additionally, I found that having menu selectors lead to other menu selectors wasn't ideal for this project. The dropdown menu selectors proved misleading in practice, with sections dedicated to oysters and the bar inadvertently leading users down an inconvenient click path. Observing user behavior, it became evident that users often found themselves on the oyster or bar about pages when their intent was to navigate to the corresponding menu pages.

03.

The final challenge identified pertained to the organization of menu content, specifically concerning the kid's menu, dietary-inclusive options, and visual representation through photos. Users encountered difficulties navigating through these aspects within the existing menu layout. This raised concerns about the clarity and accessibility of information, prompting a need for a reevaluation of the menu structure.

Solutions Found

UX Changes

To tackle the challenge of users struggling to locate specific menus, a comprehensive redesign of the dropdown menu was undertaken. The aim was to enhance compactness and clarity, facilitating a more intuitive user experience. Notably, the decision was made to relocate the oysters about page from the menu, integrating it seamlessly into the "Our Fresh Ingredients" section. Also strategic consolidation of all STIR's menus into a sleek two by three grid significantly optimized the user interface. The outcome was a more efficient and visually appealing menu layout, providing users with an enhanced platform to effortlessly explore a variety of delectable options. As part of this improvement, consideration was given to implementing an overflow function. When activated, this feature is envisioned to reveal the remaining two time-specific menus, adding an extra layer of convenience for users seeking specific culinary delights.

Design Changes

To infuse the website with a vibrant touch, I incorporated distinctive icons for each menu selector. This not only injected a burst of color but also served to enhance visual appeal. The design aimed to strike a balance between dimensionality and the clean aesthetics of flat design, resulting in buttons that exuded a sense of depth without compromising simplicity.

Furthermore, to elevate user engagement, I introduced dynamic active styling to the menu items. This not only provided a visually pleasing experience but also offered users additional cues about their current selection. The harmonious blend of color, icons, and interactive elements contributed to a more engaging and informative user interface, enhancing the overall browsing experience.

Creative Solutions

Recognizing the challenges faced by the host stand with a constant influx of inquiries about wait times, I envisioned an innovative solution to enhance customer experience. Drawing inspiration from the Top Golf model, I proposed the integration of an estimated wait time function on the website.

This feature not only serves as a valuable source of real-time information for users but also alleviates the burden on the host stand. By providing guests with an estimate of the wait time, we aim to streamline the communication process, allowing hosts to dedicate more attention to fostering positive customer relations. This thoughtful addition not only improves operational efficiency but also contributes to a more seamless and informative experience for patrons.
